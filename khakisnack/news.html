September 14, 2001 Who the hell would be reading this?!?! Well, we're done folks. It's been an insane ride, but alas, the Great Rock'n'Roll Swindle Part 2 seems to have finally come to an end. First, the really long boring part. We played our last show and it was crazy. Not because we were sounding good or anything, just because it felt freaking amazing. Chris, essentially TKS's founder, made his triumphant non-costume wearing return to the stage to play bass for "My Very Own Flag". After the heartfelt performance "Mr.T" hung out on stage while we pretty much did all the Snack classics(ie almost everything we had ever done), from the loved and hated "...Blender..." to the always screwed up "P.S..."....covers of "Like a Prayer/420 club", a new one time only song called "The Marco Song", the hip hop masterpiece known as "Dolla Dolla Bill Ya'll", some other carp i can't remember because it was in june and of course, the infamous TKS Anthem "Barnone...Worst Christmas Ever". We closed by playing some of "Happy Bunny/Pie Are Squared"(early snack metal...death metal) then closed with the signature "Robbie Alomar Jr/Wyclef Jean" We said bye, sold a whack of cd's, spent every cent we made that night and it was done...kinda. I still have a pile of CD's if anyone wants one...it's cheap and contains sontent from Saved By The Bell...khakisnack@hotmail.com. Also, Dave and i are still kinda writing crap. Actually, one of my favourite songs we've ever done is called "I'd Sell My Liver For Just A Piece Of Your Heart"...we had the idea for a long time, but this summer one ngiht, we changed around the guitar part, wrote all the words, and now it's a half decent song. What does that mean? I don't know. But i have class and can't finish this now, so peace, i'll try to get pics up soon...it's only been over a year...later

February 18, 2001 Hi. Well, we played the show on Friday, and i must say it was aaaaaaaaaaalright. It was a pretty wacky evening. We show up thinking were are gonna play at 9 or so. After sitting on our duffs for a while they tell us we have 7:30. No one we know would have made it. So, after much yammering and discussion we traded timeslots with a Croatian guy that had long hair. Now we're on at 10:50. Which causes a lot more confusion. Anyway, we went to Subway, obviously avoiding the "Sub Nazi" that works at the Mr. Sub there, and had delicious sandwiches. I wish i was eating one right now. Then we went to some other place...the black bull or something...and talked about worldly matters while dave watched foxy boxing. But i digress...the show ended up being pretty fun. We were grunge, goth, hick and vexus all at once...and that's just what it looked like. Not to mention a memorable appearance by a large mascot sized bear, some breakdancing...and then seeing Chris the Bear get stolen. We played two new songs. They were a little shaky....so i forgot a few lyrics....but they were still pretty fun to do. Opened with an unrecognizable cover of Forgot About Dre and did all the snack usuals including a souped up version of Dolla Dolla Bill Ya'll (Paul=Good) We even played an encore much to the displeasure of our friends at Supernova. I enjoyed being kindly escorted off the stage after greatly exceeding our alotted time. At this time i want to say a very very big thank you to everyone who came out to the show. I mean, the only reason it was any good was because of the high number of friends...and AT A SUPERNOVA SHOW. We understand ten bucks is a lot, and when people who don't like this kind of music show up, and people from Owen Sound(!) show up, it's beyond hype. We have already received pictures and we have both audio and video tape of the show. Pictures will be up soon and we are going to post some mp3's despite their lack of quality just for kicks. It was fun. The next one will be better.

February 11,2000 Khaki Snack hit the stage of the Reverb at around 7:40. Much to their confusion, quite a crowd had gathered. Other than the fact that Chris missed the soundcheck, we thought we were good to go. Ian and Dave played Handel's Royal fireworks as entrance music for Chris and I. The audience showed their appreciation with healthy cries of "YOU SUCK". The next logical thing to do was play the chorus from the great latin sensation Ricky Martin's Cup Of Life. After the traditional massacre of a Less Than Jake classic, we moved on to perform Chris Got a Blender for Christmas for the first time ever. This was followed by the return of the notorious 80's hit "Luft Balloons", some more Less Than Jake and then the band's favourite, "Robbie Alomar Is Your Father Henceforth Making You Robbie Alomar Jr." From there, we played the Snack Pack's fav, our rap song: "Dolla' Dolla' Bill Ya'll"(which featured members of Laced, the 4:20 Club, Two Stroke Finish, Punkdonalds, and Mugshot), then the last Less Than Jake cover, and finished off with our tribute to mathematics, love, and hardcore insanity "Pie Are Squared". Throw in a wig, a fitted doves and roses shirt, some blue hair, and plenty of pelvic thrusts into the crowd and you've got our show. Pictures and video footage will be posted as soon as possible, by which i mean, whenever i feel like it.
